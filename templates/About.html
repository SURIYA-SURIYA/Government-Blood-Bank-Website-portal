Who Can Donate Blood

To donate blood, individuals typically must be at least 16 or 17 years old, depending on local regulations, with some blood banks allowing 16-year-olds to donate with parental consent. Donors should be in good health at the time of donation, meaning they should not have any acute illnesses, infections, or chronic health conditions that could affect the donation process. A minimum weight of 110 pounds (50 kg) is often required to ensure the donor can safely give blood without adverse effects. Additionally, it is advised to avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours prior to donating, as alcohol consumption can lead to dehydration and affect both the quality of the blood collected and the donor’s recovery.

Avoid Alcohol Before Donation

Donors are advised to avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours prior to donating blood.

Healthy Lifestyle

Donors should maintain a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and adequate hydration.
